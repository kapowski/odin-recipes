Banh Mi

For as long as I have lived in Vancouver the banh mi sandwich has been my go to cheap convenient food and its mix of pickled veggies, meat, pate and spicy peppers has cemented itself as my favourite sandwich. Due to its affordability (and my proximity to an excellent banh mi spot) I have never tried this recipe, and likely never will. However, for those of you in the unfortunate position of not living within 3 blocks of a place that serves banh mi this recipe looks legit.

Ingredients (makes 4)

  • 4 crusty long bread rolls
  • 6 tbsp pork or chicken pate
  • 6 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 4 - 8 slices Thi Nguoi ("pink ham") OR brawn
  • 4 - 8 slices Cha Lua Vietnamese pork loaf OR chicken loaf
  • 4 - 8 slices roast or grilled pork cold cuts
  • 1.5 cups fresh coriander/cilantro sprigs
  • 2 cucumbers
  • 4 green onion stems
  • 2 red chillies
  • 2 tsp Maggi Seasoning
  • 4 medium carrots
  • 1 1/2 cups (375ml) hot water
  • 1/2 cup (100g) white sugar
  • 4 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup (185ml) rice wine vinegar (sub apple cider vinegar)

Steps

  1. Split rolls down the centre of the top.
  2. Spread 1.5 tbsp pate on one side, then 1.5 tbsp mayonnaise on top.
  3. Layer in the hams, cucumber slices and green onion.
  4. Stuff in plenty of carrots and coriander sprigs.
  5. Sprinkle with fresh chilli - as much as you dare!
  6. Drizzle with Maggi Seasoning (about 1/2 tsp per roll).
  7. Close sandwich together and devour!
